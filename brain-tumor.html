01 Overview

What the Project Does

Brain tumors represent one of the most life-threatening medical conditions. Manual MRI analysis by radiologists is time-consuming and prone to human variability. This project presents an integrated deep-learning diagnostic system that combines three powerful CNN architectures into a soft-voting ensemble to accurately detect and classify brain tumors from MRI scans.

The four tumor classes classified are Glioma, Meningioma, Pituitary, and No Tumor — achieving an ensemble accuracy of 99.01%.

Critically, the system integrates Explainable AI via Grad-CAM, overlaying heatmaps on MRI images to show which tumor regions influenced each prediction — building clinical trust and transparency.

Problem Statement

Early-stage brain tumors exhibit subtle visual patterns, making manual detection error-prone. Single CNN models may overfit or show bias. Most AI systems in healthcare are "black boxes" — providing predictions without clinical explanation.

Solution

Ensemble of 3 CNNs using soft-voting reduces individual model bias. Grad-CAM provides visual explanations, making the system interpretable and trustworthy for radiologists. Achieves near-perfect accuracy with clinical explainability.

03 Models

CNN Architectures

Three state-of-the-art CNN architectures were selected for their complementary strengths. Each was individually trained, fine-tuned, and evaluated before being combined in the ensemble.

🔗
DenseNet201
97.4%
Dense skip-connections for superior feature reuse. 35 epochs (25 base + 10 fine-tune). Best in low-level texture capture. Excels at No Tumor class (100%).
🔀
InceptionV3
~97%
Multi-scale parallel convolution branches. Captures small tumors and irregular shapes. 30 epochs. Strong performance in Pituitary & Meningioma classes.
⚡
EfficientNetB3
~99%
Compound scaling of depth, width & resolution. Best individual generalization. ~35 epochs. Superior consistency with minimal misclassification.

Model Comparison

04 Explainability

Grad-CAM Heatmaps

Deep learning models in healthcare must be explainable. Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping) generates visual heatmaps showing which regions of the MRI scan influenced the model's prediction.

Bright red/yellow regions indicate highest model attention. For tumor cases, heatmaps consistently focus on pathological tissue — validating clinical correctness and building radiologist trust.

Why Explainability Matters
  • → Clinicians need to verify AI focuses on tumor regions
  • → Regulatory compliance in medical AI requires transparency
  • → Helps identify incorrect model focus in misclassified cases
  • → Increases clinical adoption of AI systems
